### Current Status of the 2026 Iran Conflict
As of March 12, 2026, the US-Israel war with Iran, which began with surprise airstrikes on February 28 targeting Iranian missiles, air defenses, and leadership (including Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei), has entered its second week of intense escalation.[1][2] Key developments include US strikes on Iranian naval vessels and minelayers in the Strait of Hormuz, Iranian missile barrages on Israel, attacks on shipping, and strikes on Tehran's oil depots.[3][4] Iran has named Mojtaba Khamenei as the new Supreme Leader, amid reports of degraded missile/drone capabilities.[5][6] The conflict risks disrupting global energy supplies via the Strait of Hormuz.

### Investment Impacts
#### Oil
The conflict has driven sharp gains in oil prices due to supply disruptions—nearly 20% of global crude and gas affected—and OPEC cuts by Saudi Arabia et al. Brent has surged past $100/bbl (up ~7% in recent sessions to 2022 highs), with analysts warning of $91+ by late 2026 or higher if Hormuz blockades worsen; fuel panics are spreading in Asia.[7][8][9] **Investment angle**: Bullish for oil producers/ETFs (e.g., USO, XLE); prolonged war could sustain highs but risks stagflation.[10]

#### Gold
Gold has been volatile as a safe-haven: spiked to $5,400/oz early on, hit $5,246 amid initial panic, but pulled back below $5,100 due to a stronger USD and inflation fears from rising energy costs; currently ~$5,120.[11][12][13] Forecasts remain bullish (e.g., J.P. Morgan at $6,300 EOY), but dollar strength caps upside.[14] **Investment angle**: Attractive for hedges (GLD, miners), but watch USD/oil interplay.

#### Defense Stocks
Defense names have outperformed amid munitions burn and expected budget surges: RTX +110%, Northrop Grumman +60%, General Dynamics +57%, Lockheed Martin +37%, with Palantir also rising; recent stumbles tied to broad selloffs.[15][16][17] FY2026 budgets already include $20B+ for stockpiles.[18] **Investment angle**: Strong tailwinds (ITA ETF, LMT, RTX); "forever war" fears boost sector despite volatility.[19]

#### Broader Stock Market
Markets are under pressure: global stocks falling, S&P 500 down ~1.5% YTD (average stock +3.2%), with inflation/stagflation risks from oil spikes dominating; some muted reactions as AI hype offsets geopolitics, but vessel attacks deepen declines.[20][21][22] Trump signals quick end, but analysts see 2-week limit.[23] **Investment angle**: Risk-off; energy/defense winners, tech/energy-sensitive losers; VIX elevated.
